Who We Are

About USDF

Udyam Social Development Foundation (USDF) is a non-profit, non-governmental organization established in 2022 at Kakodonga, Golaghat, Assam, India. Registered under the Societies Registration Act, USDF is dedicated to promoting sustainable development and improving the quality of life of communities through education, skill development, environmental conservation, women empowerment, health awareness, and social welfare initiatives.

Since its inception, the Foundation has been working at the grassroots level to address social challenges and create opportunities for inclusive growth. Through collaboration with government agencies, educational institutions, corporate partners, and local communities, USDF strives to bring meaningful and lasting change to society.

Our Journey

Our History

From a shared vision to a movement for change — tracing the milestones that shaped USDF.

Udyam Social Development Foundation was founded with the belief that sustainable development begins with empowered communities. Recognizing the diverse challenges faced by rural and underserved populations, a group of dedicated social workers and community leaders came together to establish an organization that would work towards holistic social development.

Over the years, USDF has implemented numerous community-based initiatives, including environmental awareness campaigns, plantation drives, educational support programs, cultural and youth development activities, women empowerment projects, and community welfare programs. These efforts have strengthened the Foundation's reputation as a committed and responsible development organization in Assam.

2022

Foundation Established

USDF was formally established at Kakodonga, Golaghat, Assam, registered under the Societies Registration Act with a vision for holistic community development.

2023

Community Programs

Launched grassroots community welfare initiatives, educational support programs, and cultural development activities across Golaghat district.

2024

Environmental Campaigns

Organized large-scale environmental awareness campaigns, plantation drives, and climate action programs to protect and preserve the natural ecosystem.

2025

Women Empowerment

Strengthened women empowerment through skill development, entrepreneurship promotion, self-help group formation, and sustainable livelihood initiatives.

2026

Youth Development

Expanded youth leadership programs, digital literacy drives, vocational training, and capacity-building activities for the next generation of changemakers.
