About Udyam

Born from the Heart of Golaghat

Founded in 2022, Udyam Social Development Foundation emerged from a simple belief: that local problems deserve local solutions, driven by people who have lived them. We are young, we are rooted, and we are relentless.

We operate at the intersection of education, community welfare, and youth empowerment — building a Golaghat where ambition finds a path and every child has a fair start.

Our Work

What We Do

Three pillars of action, one unified mission: a more equitable Golaghat.

01

Youth Empowerment & Guidance

Turning ambition into action — one young person at a time.

🛠️
Mentorship & Skill Training

Hands-on workshops that bridge formal education and real-world job readiness, giving local youth the practical toolkit they need to compete.

💰
Financial Literacy

Budgeting, saving, and financial planning sessions designed to break cycles of instability and build a foundation for economic independence.

🧠
Mental Health Awareness

Counseling resources, peer support groups, and motivational sessions to build emotional resilience in young people facing real pressure.

02

Basic Education & Child Support

No child left behind because of poverty.

📚
Learning Resources

We supply books, stationery, and tutoring support to children from marginalized backgrounds who are at risk of dropping out due to expenses.

🏛️
Community Libraries

Organizing neighbourhood libraries — quiet, resourceful spaces where children can study, read, and discover a world beyond their circumstances.

03

Community Welfare & Infrastructure

Improving everyday life at the ground level.

🏥
Health & Sanitization Drives

Free health check-up camps for low-income families and awareness campaigns on clean water access and basic sanitation practices.

👩
Women Empowerment Networks

Organizing local women into self-reliance circles, connecting them with small-scale livelihood skills and peer support systems.

🐾
Animal Welfare & Civic Duties

Volunteer-led drives for local stray animal care, rescue missions, and cultural and environmental preservation programs across the district.
